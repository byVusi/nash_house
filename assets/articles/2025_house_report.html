What is Nash House?

Mila Xego, Head of House


On the surface Nash house is a boarding and day-boy house that was Established in 1907 being named after The Rt Rev James Okey Nash who was Headmaster of St John's College from 1906-1917 and Coadjutor Bishop of Cape Town from 1917-1930.

However those in Nash know that the House is much more than what was just stated. It is more than a house within the St Johns House System or a boarding house. Nash house is a community, a family and a legacy that every College Boy, that is lucky enough to call himself a “Nashtonian”, carries with an immense sense of pride. To be a part of Nash means that you are a part of something bigger than yourself, something that demands dedication, loyalty and spirit.

The words “A dream for many, a reality for few”, which are closely related to the house, captures exactly what it means to wear the iconic blue and white Nash colours. To be in Nash is not about the bare minimum - it is about living up to the standards, traditions and values that make the House not just any other House but Nash house. Many people dream of being a part of something so full and rich with heritage and culture, but only a few have the honour of calling themselves a Nashtonian. But as the saying goes “With great power (honour) comes great responsibility”: to give your best, to support your fellow Nashtonians and uphold the pride of the house in everything we do.

The house Motto, “Conando Vincimus” (By striving, we conquer) is a constant reminder that success does not come easily just because one is in Nash House. Success is earned through hard work, persistence and resilience. Every Nashtonian needs to understand that victories are forged in the countless hours of effort and preparation behind the scenes. We conquer not only through competition, but through the character and unity we show along through our journey through the College.

To be a Nashtonian is to embrace this spirit: the courage to dream, the strength to strive and the will to overcome challenges. Nash house is where individuals become part of a family, where struggles and challenges turn into opportunities and where success (no matter how small) belongs to every single Nashtonian.

Personal achievements

Nashtonians are well known for their high level of achievements within the College, whether it be playing in a first team or earning colours and honours. However, this year, a few Nashtonians soared above all expectations and made a significant impact on provincial and national stages. Allan Njangfang Patu (UIV), who played both first team basketball and for the mighty Blues despite only being in UIV, was selected to represent the Golden Lions U17 Welpies side and was named to the BNL Schools All-Star Team. Similarly, basketball phenom Ama Ntsubane (LV) also earned a place on the BNL Schools All-Star Team following his many stellar performances for the Blues on the court. Young Max Baxter (REM) took the competitive shooting world by storm, defeating the UK's top two ranked marksmen and narrowly missing first place in an under-16 tournament, despite being only 13 years old at the time.Congratulations also go to Alec Loveland for earning 100 caps for the 1st Team cricket side, joining Nashtonian legends Joe Macrobert and Devon Conway as only the 13th player in College history to reach this remarkable milestone. This achievement stands as a testament to Alec's dedication, skill, and consistency at the highest level of College cricket.

Some notable achievements from a House perspective

Over this past year, the spirit of competition truly ignited the light of Nashtonian Brotherhood like never before. One of the most memorable new traditions was born as the boarders gathered in the common room to learn and practice new war cries in preparation for IH swimming, especially the highly contested Spirit Cup. While this lively noise was at times a disturbance to neighboring houses and stooges, it undeniably fostered a strong sense of camaraderie and unity that deserves high commendation. Nash House has long been known for its rugby prowess, and after a challenging start in all Inter-House competitions, rugby became our chance to claim a first win. The boys rose to the occasion, securing our first IH competition victory, which filled the entire house with joy and excitement. This triumph marked the beginning of what the boys affectionately called “la Remontada,” or the comeback, as we went on to win IH hockey, IH rugby, and IH athletics despite a tough year.

Another highlight was the Inter-House drama performance, where Nash came 2nd overall. Lehakoe Matlala (LV) wrote the script, which won Best Original Script, and Makutuma Mothabela (LV) directed the production. Cast members Tiri Pasipanodya earned Overall Best Actor, while Koketso and Thato Phatudi won Best Duo, showcasing the amazing talent and creativity within Nash House.

However, not all results were as successful. Finishing 9th in Inter-House Music and 7th in Inter-House Swimming was a disappointment, falling short of the standards Nash House strives for. These placements served as a reminder that while our strengths shone in certain areas, there remains important work to be done to improve and elevate all aspects of our House's performance. Despite these setbacks, the unwavering support and brotherhood that grew from the successes helped keep morale high and inspired us to aim even higher next year. The collective spirit extended far beyond the competition, bonding the boarding house and the wider College community in pride and unity.

Leadership

House prefects: Mila Xego (HOH), Luke Brooks (Deputy HoH), Vashiv Naidoo, Oliver Kirk, Nicholas Morrell, Noah Xego.

In 2025, Nash House proudly had four School Prefects, including Custos Kamogelo Thobejane, Second Prefect Menzi Bongwe, and Head of School Siza Gule, all distinguished Nashotonians. Throughout the year, the house prefects worked tirelessly, dedicating themselves fully to serving and supporting the boys of Nash House. A standout moment was their heartfelt project to honour Nash House legend Joel Leotlela, who earned the prestigious distinction of wearing the iconic Green and Gold for the Junior Boks and was later selected for the BlitzBoks squad. To celebrate his remarkable achievements, the Prefects arranged for a framed game-worn jersey to be displayed prominently in the Common Room, ensuring his legacy continues to inspire all Nash boys. It is with immense pride that we announce the 2026 leadership team for Nash House, with Simon Musset appointed as Head of School and Ama Ntusbane as Custos, both distinguished Nash boys.

Simon Mussett and Christopher Chapman, the latter being named Head of Nash House for 2026, have demonstrated exceptional leadership, particularly in water polo, while Simon also excelled on the first team rugby squad. Ama has been an outstanding athlete across multiple sports including basketball, rugby, hockey, and football, showcasing remarkable versatility and dedication. Beyond their athletic achievements, all three have made significant contributions to their Nash House portfolios, exemplifying the true spirit of leadership and commitment that Nash House stands for.

We extend our deepest gratitude to our Housemaster, Mr Mathonsi, and The Assistant Housemaster, Mr Mattiuzzo, for their outstanding leadership and unwavering commitment to Nash House throughout the year. Their tireless dedication has been the cornerstone in shaping a nurturing and supportive environment where every boy feels respected, valued, and safe. Both have gone beyond the call of duty not only in guiding and mentoring the prefects but also in reaching out to every single member of the house, ensuring that no one feels overlooked or unsupported. Mr Mathonsi and Mr Mattiuzzo have created a culture of openness and trust, making Nash House not just a residence, but a true home for all its boys. Their encouragement, patience, and genuine care have fostered an atmosphere where individuals can develop their talents, build strong friendships, and grow into confident young men. Thanks to their leadership, the house has become a place where camaraderie, respect, and personal growth are celebrated every day. We are truly fortunate to have such dedicated role models who lead by example and continuously inspire all Nash House members to strive for excellence while enjoying their time together in a safe and welcoming community.

A dream for many, a reality for few. Conando Vincimus
