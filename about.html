About Nash House

Welcome to Nash House

In Nash House, you're more than a student - you're family. With just over 100 boys, our house is a close-knit community where every member is known, supported, and encouraged to thrive. Whether you're a day-boy or a boarder, Nash House is a space where your talents, passions, and potential are celebrated.

Our Housemaster, along with a dedicated team of tutors and resident tutors, is here to guide and support you. From academics to sports, culture to leadership, Nash House is a place where you'll grow, build lifelong friendships, and discover the best version of yourself. Welcome Home!

Our Foundation

Nash House is steeped in history and inspired by The Rt Rev James Okey Nash CR MA DD (Oxon), who led St John's College as Headmaster from 1906 to 1917 and later served as the Coadjutor Bishop of Cape Town.

James Nash first stepped into St John's College in 1905, initially to teach Divinity. But his passion for education quickly led him to lend a hand in Latin, French, and English, proving that his dedication knew no bounds.

Originally, he was meant to be here for just a six-month trial. However, by September 1905, it was clear that Nash was destined for greatness, as he was appointed Headmaster starting January 1, 1906.

In his first year as Headmaster, James Nash introduced the House system, giving rise to Nash, Rakers (which later became Hill), Thomson, and Alston. His vision for a vibrant community began to take shape!

But it wasn't just about academics for James Nash; he was deeply committed to finding a permanent home for St John's College, one that would not only meet the needs of the present but also accommodate his dreams for the future. Thanks to his relentless pursuit, we are now proud to call our current premises home.

We celebrate the legacy of James Nash and the vibrant spirit of Nash House, where tradition and vision come together to create an extraordinary community!

Our Symbols

Our crest and house colour have become deeply ingrained in our traditions and embody the pride, unity, and resilience that define us.

The Nash House Crest

The crest consists of three Kori Bustards, each with a laurel twig in their beaks. The birds are placed on a blue shield.

Our House Colour

Our house colour is lightblue. We wear our lightblue t-shirts at most of our Inter-House events when participating or supporting.

House Motto

Conando Vincimus, which translates to "By striving, we conquer", is more than just a phrase - it's a way of life. It reflects a commitment to resilience, ambition, and excellence in every endeavour.

Whether on the sports field, in the classroom, or within the community, Nashtonians embody this spirit, pushing beyond limits and rising to challenges.
